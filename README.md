## Project Overview
This project applies structured SQL analysis to the Telco Customer Churn public dataset to answer real-world business questions across five analytical domains, from revenue aggregation to A/B test simulation and statistical lift calculation.

The **goal** is to simulate the analytical workflow of a product/growth data analyst at a SaaS or telecom company:
- clean the data
- formulate business hypotheses
- write production-grade queries
- surface actionable insights
  
| Focus Area | Questions |
|---|---|
| Customer Base & Revenue Composition | Revenue by contract type, revenue concentration, customer lifetime distribution |
| Churn & Retention Analytics | Global churn rate, early churn detection, high-risk segment discovery |
| Pricing & Revenue Analytics | Price sensitivity by quintile, upsell opportunity, annualized revenue at risk |
| Product Adoption & Feature Analytics | Feature adoption impact, bundle effect, digital maturity segmentation |
| Experimentation & A/B Testing | Discount offer simulation, onboarding intervention, statistical lift calculation |

## Dataset
Source: [Telco Customer Churn — IBM Sample Dataset via Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/blastchar/telco-customer-churn).
 
The dataset contains approximately 7,000 customer records from a fictional US telecom provider. It includes information on demographics, subscribed services, contract type, payment method, monthly and total charges, tenure, and churn status.

## Data Cleaning
Before running any analysis, raw data was reviewed to ensure consistency and reliability.
 
Key transformations applied:
- Converted `TotalCharges` from string to numeric, handling blank values for customers with zero tenure
- Standardized binary categorical columns (`Yes`/`No`) for consistent filtering and aggregation
- Excluded records with null or zero `MonthlyCharges` from revenue-focused queries
- Verified referential integrity across customer, service, and contract dimensions

## Analysis Structure
 
### Customer Base & Revenue Composition
 
- **Revenue Composition by Contract Type**: Calculates customer count, ARPU (Average Price Per Unit), and total revenue per contract type (month-to-month, one-year, two-year), ordered by revenue contribution.
- **Revenue Concentration**: Uses window functions to measure what share of total revenue is generated by the top 10%, 20%, and 50% of customers — surfacing the degree of value concentration in the base.
- **Customer Lifetime Distribution**: Segments customers into tenure buckets (0–6, 6–12, 12–24, 24–48, 48+ months) and calculates ARPU and churn rate per cohort, exposing the relationship between tenure and customer value.

### Churn & Retention Analytics
 
- **Global Churn Rate**: Computes overall churn rate broken down by contract type and payment method, identifying the highest- and lowest-risk combinations.
- **Early Churn Detection**: Measures the percentage of churned customers who left within 3, 6, and 12 months — a standard SaaS metric for evaluating onboarding effectiveness.
- **High-Risk Segment Discovery**: Identifies the top 5 customer segments with the highest churn rate by combining contract type, internet service type, and payment method into a single cross-dimensional view.
### Pricing & Revenue Analytics
 
- **Price Sensitivity Analysis**: Divides customers into spending quintiles using `NTILE(5)` and calculates churn rate and ARPU per quintile, revealing the non-linear relationship between price and retention.
- **Upsell Opportunity Identification**: Flags long-tenure customers (>24 months) paying below the median monthly charge as a high-potential cohort for plan upgrades.
- **Annualized Revenue at Risk**: Calculates `monthly_charge × 12` for all churned customers, segmented by contract type, to quantify the top-line impact of churn per segment.
### Product Adoption & Feature Analytics
 
- **Feature Adoption Impact**: Computes churn rate and customer count for each add-on service (Online Security, Tech Support, Streaming TV, Streaming Movies), isolating which features correlate with better retention.
- **Bundle Effect**: Compares churn rate between customers with 3 or more add-on services and those with fewer, providing evidence for or against a bundle-first growth strategy.
- **Digital Maturity Segmentation**: Segments customers by total number of active services and calculates average tenure, churn rate, and ARPU per segment — linking product adoption depth to long-term customer value.
### Experimentation & A/B Testing
 
- **A/B Test Simulation — Discount Offer**: Assigns customers to two groups via hash-based modulo (`MOD(customer_id_hash, 2)`) and compares churn rate and ARPU between a control group and a simulated 10% discount group.
- **A/B Test — Onboarding Intervention**: Isolates new customers (<6 months tenure), splits them into control and treatment groups, and compares churn rates to evaluate the hypothetical impact of an improved onboarding experience.
- **Statistical Lift Calculation**: Computes the relative lift between treatment and control groups, outputting control rate, treatment rate, absolute difference, and lift, applying standard experiment evaluation methodology.
## Key Insights
 
- 📉 **Month-to-month customers on electronic check churn at 53.7%** : the riskiest segment in the entire base, more than 50 percentage points above the safest cohort.
- 💰 **The top 20% of customers generate 54% of total revenue** : the bottom 50% contribute just 11%, making revenue concentration a critical strategic risk.
- ⏱️ **56.8% of churned customers leave within the first 3 months** : if a customer survives the first year, churn probability drops to 17%. Onboarding is everything.
- 📦 **Customers with 3+ add-on services churn at 8%**, compared to 18% for low-adoption customers. Bundles are the single strongest retention lever in the dataset.
- 📈 **ARPU grows 33x from the lowest to the highest tenure bucket** ($143 at 0–6 months vs. $4,685 at 48+ months). Long-term customers are not just more loyal, they are categorically more valuable.
- 🧪 **A 10% discount did not reduce churn** in the simulated experiment, while also lowering ARPU by ~$200. Indiscriminate discounting destroys margin without improving retention.
- ⚠️ **$1.45M in annualized revenue is at risk from month-to-month churn alone** : nearly 9x the exposure of one-year and two-year contracts combined.
